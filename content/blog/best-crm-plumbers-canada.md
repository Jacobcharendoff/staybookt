# The 5 Best CRMs for Canadian Plumbers in 2026 (Honest Comparison)

You're losing money right now. Not on purpose. But every lead that slips through the cracks, every customer who gets forgotten, every job that doesn't get scheduled fast enough—that's revenue walking out the door.

Most Canadian plumbers run their business on sticky notes and text messages. It works for a while. Then you hire your first employee, your second job site overlaps with the third, and suddenly nobody knows who called when or what they need.

A good CRM fixes this. It catches every lead. It reminds you to follow up. It tracks what each customer paid last time. It makes sure nothing falls through the cracks.

## What Actually Matters in a Plumbing CRM

Before we compare the tools, let's be clear about what you actually need:

**Ease of use.** Your team won't use software they don't understand. If it takes 20 minutes to log a job, they'll go back to pen and paper. You need something your plumber can use on a phone in the van without a manual.

**Canadian tax support.** GST. HST. PST. QST. Different provinces, different rates. Your CRM should calculate it automatically or you're doing extra work. US-built tools get this wrong.

**Mobile first.** Your plumbers work in the field. They need to update a job, send an invoice, take a photo—all from their phone. Web-only doesn't cut it.

**Price that makes sense.** You're running a plumbing business, not a tech company. You need features that pay for themselves, not features that sound cool.

**Speed to lead response.** First responder wins 78% of the time. Your CRM should make it easy to respond to a lead within 15 minutes, not 2 hours.
