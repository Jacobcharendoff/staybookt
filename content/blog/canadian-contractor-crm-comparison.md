# Why Canadian Contractors Need a CRM Built for Canada (Not the US)

You're a contractor in Canada. You probably use some form of CRM—maybe it's a spreadsheet, maybe it's a tool you found online.

And almost certainly, it was built by Americans for Americans.

Here's the problem: American software doesn't solve Canadian problems.

The tax system is different. The language requirements are different. The compliance rules are different. The way you price jobs is different. When you force an American tool to work for your Canadian business, you're doing extra work every single day. Work that shouldn't exist.

Let's walk through why this matters and what it actually costs you.

## The Problem: Most CRMs Are Built for the US Market

When you pick a CRM, you're not just picking software. You're picking someone's understanding of how a contractor business works. And if that understanding is based on US tax law, US industry practices, and US customers, it's going to feel wrong in Canada.

It'll work. But it'll be work.

## The Canadian Contractor's Real Problem: Tax Complexity

Let's talk about GST, HST, PST, and QST.

In the US, there's sales tax. One rate per state, mostly. It's simple.

In Canada, you have:
- **GST:** 5% federally (applies everywhere)
- **HST:** 13–15% (Ontario, Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, Newfoundland & Labrador, PEI)
- **PST:** 7–10% (British Columbia, Saskatchewan, Manitoba)
- **QST:** 5% + GST (Quebec, plus special rules for contractor work)

And here's where it gets weird: the rules change depending on what you're doing and where you're doing it.

If you're a plumber in Ontario doing a repair, it's HST. If you're doing new construction, some items might be exempt. If you're working in Quebec, the rules are different. If you're a residential contractor, the rules are different again.

An American CRM has no idea. You end up doing the math yourself, which means:
- Mistakes (undercharging by accident, or overcharging and losing customers)
- Extra time (someone on your team recalculating every invoice)
- Compliance risk (if you get the tax wrong, you're responsible to the CRA)

A Canadian CRM handles this automatically. It knows which province you're in, what type of work you're doing, and which tax rules apply. It calculates it right the first time.

## The Language Problem: Bill 96 and Customer Expectations

In Quebec, Bill 96 tightened French language requirements for businesses. In practice, it means French language support isn't optional—it's expected.

But even outside Quebec, contractor customers increasingly expect bilingual communication. Invoices in French. Job descriptions in French. Follow-up messages in French. It's not a luxury, it's an expectation.

An American CRM gives you English only. If you need French, you're hiring a translator, rewriting everything, or looking unprofessional to a segment of your customers.

A Canadian CRM supports both languages natively. Your team can switch between English and French. Customer communications come through in the right language. Nobody has to work around the system.

## The Compliance Headache

Canadian contractors deal with compliance rules that American software doesn't understand:
- **CRA reporting:** Your revenue numbers need to match your business tax records
- **GST registration:** If you're GST-registered (and most contractors are), your CRM needs to track taxable vs. non-taxable sales separately
- **Deduction tracking:** You need to track which expenses are deductible and organize them for tax season
- **Wage compliance:** If you have employees, there are Canadian payroll rules the CRM should understand

American software doesn't handle these. You end up exporting data to spreadsheets and doing manual reconciliation with your accountant.

A Canadian CRM integrates with Canadian tax and payroll systems. It speaks the language of your accountant. Invoices, revenue tracking, and expense categories are all organized the way the CRA expects them.
